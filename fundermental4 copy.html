FUNDAMENTAL BELIEF 4:
GOD THE SON

God the eternal Son became incarnate in Jesus Christ. Through Him all things were created, the character of God is revealed, the salvation of humanity is accomplished, and the world is judged. Forever truly God, He became also truly man, Jesus the Christ. He was conceived of the Holy Spirit and born of the virgin Mary. He lived and experienced temptation but did not fall into sin. He suffered and died voluntarily on the cross for our sins, was raised from the dead and ascended to minister in the heavenly sanctuary in our behalf. He will come again in glory for the final deliverance of His people and the restoration of all things. Amen!

God the Son, the incarnate came into this earth to bring salvation to humanity just like the bronze serpent in the wilderness. Numbers 21:9; John 3:14,15.







He became sin, taking upon Himself all the sins of everyone who has lived or will live. 2 Cor.5:21. The plan to save man was laid way before the foundation of the world and Jesus offered to die for sinful man to bring us back to God. 1 Peter 1:20,21; Gen 3:15.
Both Jesus and Satan would be scathed in the controversy between good and evil! But victory would be for God’s Son!

Jesus birth was miraculous.
Matt.1:18-23.
A Roman decree brought her to Bethehem, the predicted birth place. Luke 2:4-7. One of His names was Immanuel “God with Us’’ Matt.1:23. His common name, Jesus, focused on His mission of salvation. Matt.1:21

Many however rejected Jesus and did not recognize Him as Savior of the world. John 1:11; Luke 23:18.
Toward the end of Jesus’s 31/2 year ministry He was betrayed by His disciple for 30 pieces of silver. John 13:18; 18:2; Matt.26:14-16;
He was crucified and died. Luke 23:14,15
The incarnation of God the Son is a mystery. 1 Tim 3:16.
In trying to comprehend the incarnation, how the Creator of the worlds became a helpless babe in a manger and to wear the garb of humanity! It is well to remember Deut.29:29

Jesus Christ is truly God. Let’s look at some of the attributes of His divinity. He is omnipresent
Matt.28,20; John 17:2; Matt.18:20
but now represented by the Holy Spirit.
John 14:16-18 Jesus has immutability. Heb.13:8
He is self-existence John 11:25;
He is holy even demons testify; Mark 1:24.
He is love 1 John 3:16.
He is eternal John 1:2,3;
He is the Creator and Sustainer Col 1:16,17 Jesus’ name testifies of his divine nature. Immanuel Matt 1:23.
Both believers and demons referred to His as the Son of God Matt.8:29; Mark 1:1; 5:7.
He is also LORD of hosts John 12:41
Jesus Himself claimed equality with His Father. John 8:58. He called God My Father instead of “our father” John 10:30.
And Jesus Himself revealed God His Father as being one with Him John 14:9 Jesus was worshipped as God by both people and angels. Phil.2:10,11; Heb. 1:6. Christ reconciled humanity to God. John 1:14,18: John 17:6.
Jesus Christ is Truly Man. The Bible testifies that in addition to His divine nature, Christ has a human nature. I John 4:2,3. The acceptance of this believe is crucial. Evidence for His humanity His human development.
Luke 2:40,52.
At the age of 12 He became aware of His divine mission. Luke 2:46-49.

Throughout His childhood He was subject to His parents Luke 2:51.
Yet though He experienced development, He did not sin.

He was called a “man.” John the Baptist, Peter and Paul refer to Jesus as a Man.
John 1:30; Acts 2:22; Rom.5:15; I Tim 2:5
Christ refers to Himself as a Man John 8:40; Matt.8: 20; 26:2.
His human characteristics. Jesus was flesh and blood Heb. 2:14,17.
He was made a little lower than the angels. Ps 8:5; Heb. 2:9.
He was hungry, thirsty, tired, and anxious. He even wept. He experienced death John 19:30
and was resurrected with a body. Luke 24:36-43

Jesus Christ took human nature with all its liabilities, including the possibility of yielding to temptation Heb.4:15 but He did not fall for He depended on His Father 100%.
Our victory over temptation comes from maintaining dependence upon Him. I Cor 10:13.
Jesus took on the human nature so that: He can be our High priest or mediator between God and man. Heb.4:14-16
To reach people where they are and rescue the most hopeless. Phil 2:7 ;
To be our example. John 16:33; 13:15; 1 Peter 2:21
Jesus Christ is “one in nature, in character, in purpose” with God the Father. He truly is God! John 14:9

Have you accepted Jesus as the Son of God and Son of Man as the Savior and Lord of your life?
